Modak

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Serves: 11
Ingredients Quantity
Ghee 100 gms
Grated Fresh Coconut 200 gms
Jaggery 1 cup
Cardamom Powder 1 tsp
Rice flour 150 gms
Salt 1 pinch

Step 1: Heat a pan with 1 tsp ghee.
Step 2: Add 2 cups or 200 grams grated fresh coconut.
Step 3: Next add 1 cup jaggery.
Step 4: Stir & begin to cook on a medium flame.
Step 5: Add cardamom powder & nutmeg(optional) & set aside for cooling.
Step 6:(Making dough) Pour 1 cup water to a pot or pan. Add 1 pinch salt & 1 tsp ghee to the water. Bring it to a rolling boil.
Step 7: Turn the flame to low,add 1 cup flour(150 grams) & mix well fastly.
Step 8: The flour turns to a lumpy mixture. Cover & turn off the stove.
Step 9: When the mixture cools down to slightly hot,transfer the mixture to a wide bowl. Do not allow it to cool down completely. If needed, sprinkle hot or warm water & knead it to a smooth, crack-free & non-sticky dough.
Step 10: Divide the mixture to 11 parts. If using mould then divide according to the size of your mould.
Step 11: (with mould) Grease your mould with ghee.
Step 12: Close the mould & keep a dough ball inside. Press down the dough to the sides of the mould, making space in the centre.
Step 13: Press down the coconut filling gently. Seal the modak with the excess dough that comes out.
Step 14: Open the mould & remove the modak carefully. Always remember to keep them covered with a damp cloth until they go for steaming.
Step 15:(Without Mould) Take a bowl & shape evenly round puri. The edges have to be thinner than the centre. With the help of thumb & forefinger gently pinch off very little dough to make a pleat on the outer side.
Step 16: Place 1 to 1.5 tbsp. cooled coconut-jaggery mixture in the centre.
Step 17: Bring together all the pleats gently & seal giving a good shape.
Step 18: Steaming Modak On a medium high flame,bring 1.5 to 2 cups water to a rolling boil in a steamer. Grease the steam basket or Spread a thin cotton cloth in a wide bowl or a steam basket.
Step 19: Place the modak in the basket & steam for 10 minutes. You can sprinkle some saffron over them before steaming. Switch off & rest for a while.
Offer steamed modak to Lord Ganesha!

Kaju Katli/Cashew Fudge

  • Time: 20 mins
  • Serves: 14
Ingredients Quantity
Kaju or Cashews 160 gms
Sugar 100 gms
Water 5 tbsp
Ghee or Coconut oil(optional) 1 tbsp
Chopped rose petals or Rose water 1 tsp
Saffron(optional) 8-9 strands

Step 1 (Grinding Cashews): Powder the cashew in a coffee grinder or a dry grinder.
Step 2: The cashews should be in powdered form and not become pasty or oily.
Step 3: (Making Kaju Katli mixture) On a low flame heat sugar and water in a thick bottomed or non stick pan or kadai.
Step 4: Meanwhile grease a plate or a tray and keep aside or keep butter papers ready.
Step 5: When all the sugar gets dissolved in the water, add the cashew powder. There is no consistency in the sugar syrup required like one string or two string. Just let the sugar dissolve in the water and then proceed with the next step.
Step 6: Stir and keep on stirring the cashew mixture on a low flame.
Step 7: The cashew mixture would start thickening.
Step 8: Cook the kaju mixture for approx 7-9 minutes till the whole mixture starts to come together.
Step 9: Remove the whole lump of the kaju mixture from the pan and place it on work surface, bowl or plate.
Step 10: Add the rose petals and ghee or oil to the cashew mixture.
Step 11: When the heat in the mixture is hot enough to handle, then knead the cashew mixture.
Step 12: Flatten the dough and place it on a butter paper or on a greased plate/thali or tray.
Step 13: Place a butter paper on top and then using a rolling pin, roll gently the dough from all sides till you reach a thickness of 3-5 mm in the dough.
Step 14: Remove the butter paper and let the rolled dough cool.
Step 15: When completely cooled, using a sharp knife cut the cashew dough giving square or diamond shapes.
Step 16: Gently remove the kaju katli with a butter knife.
Step 17: Serve the kaju katli straight away or keep in an air tight container.

Besan Ke Ladoo

  • Time: 30 mins
  • Serves: 15
Ingredients Quantity
Besan(Gram flour) 200 gms
Ghee 125 gms
Powdered Sugar 175 gms
Cardamom Powder 1 tsp
Golden Raisins(Chopped)r 1-2 tbsp
Cashews(chopped) 10-12

Step 1: Dry roast the besan in a heavy kadai or pan on low to medium-low heat for about 10 to 12 minutes.
Step 2: Keep on stirring continuously right from the beginning. So that the besan is not burned and cooks evenly.
Step 3: After 12 minutes of total roasting time, add the ghee.
Step 4: Mix very well and continue roasting the besan for some more 12 to 15 minutes stirring non-stop.
Step 5: You will see that the mixture will start releasing ghee and will give a nice fragrant nutty aroma. Some ghee will float on top and and you will see a molten lava kind of consistency where the ghee is floating. The mixture will also loose sides of the pan and become one. These are the signs that the besan is fully cooked. Once you see these signs, you can move on to the next step.
Step 6: Remove the pan from the stove-top and place it on your kitchen countertop. Add powdered sugar.
Step 7: Begin to mix with all the strength from your hands. You have to mix this part vigorously so that the powdered sugar melts in the roasted besan and ghee mixture and no lumps are formed.
Step 8: Add the powdered cardamom, raisins and cashews. Mix again.
Step 9: Once you have mixed the ladoo mixture well then let it become lukewarm or cool at room temperature.
Step 10: When cooled, make small or medium-sized besan ladoo and store them in an airtight steel container. If you are not able to shape in balls then freeze the mixture for 5 minutes or refrigerate for 20 minutes. Depending on the quality and consistency of ghee as well as the room temperature you may be able to easily shape these or not.

Serve besan ke laddu garnished with some nuts!
